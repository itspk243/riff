Profile in front of you. Sequence ready to send.

No CRM. No CSV imports. No API keys. Three steps. Fifteen seconds.

Open a profile, click the icon

Works on any LinkedIn profile, plus GitHub and Wellfound. The Riffly icon in your toolbar lights up when it detects a candidate page. One click opens the popup with the profile already parsed — name, headline, role, About, pinned repos.

One sentence about why you're reaching out

"Hiring a Staff backend, payments infra, $250K + remote." That's it. Pick a tone (warm / direct / cheeky), pick a length (50w or 80w). Pasting a recent post they wrote is the single biggest quality lever — recommended but optional.

Three variants. You edit. You send.

Cold opener, follow-up, breakup — one click, all three. Riffly never types in LinkedIn for you. Copy the variant you like, paste in the conversation, edit before hitting send. Mark replies in the popup so you learn which tones are working.

Why the drafts read like you wrote them.

Four constraints, baked into every generation. The reason replies happen.

It reads what they wrote

Headline, current role, About — pulled from the profile in front of you. A pasted recent post lifts output more than any other input. The model has substance to reference, not just job titles.

A blacklist of dead phrases

"I came across your", "great fit", "passion for", "scaling challenges" — the corporate template language a model is otherwise prone to. Banned at the prompt layer, sanitized in the output.

Conversation-starter framing

One specific observation. One concrete reason it might matter to them. One question that isn't a yes/no trap. The opener doesn't pitch — it starts a conversation that earns the pitch.

No automation, ever

Riffly reads what's on screen and produces text. It never types into LinkedIn, never clicks send, never crawls in the background. You manually copy and send. That's the whole product.

"But couldn't I just paste the profile into ChatGPT?"

You could. Most people quietly do. Here's what's actually different when you stop.

ChatGPT + copy-paste
  • You manually paste whatever you remember to grab — usually just the headline. The model never sees the recent post or pinned repos.
  • The output reaches for the same template phrases ChatGPT reaches for in every other window: "I came across", "passion for", "great fit", "scaling challenges".
  • One draft. Want a follow-up? Open a new chat, re-paste, re-prompt. Want a breakup? Same again.
  • Three windows, one candidate, ten minutes. Repeat for the next forty.
Riffly
  • The extension reads what's already on screen — About, headline, current role, pinned repos — without you copy-pasting any of it.
  • The prompt bans 40+ corporate-template phrases by default. Output that drifts into them gets caught and rewritten before you see it.
  • Three variants per click — opener, follow-up, breakup. The full sequence is ready in fifteen seconds.
  • One click per candidate. Same ten minutes is now forty drafts.

Same model class as ChatGPT. Different scaffolding around it — that's the whole product.

Questions.

Will my LinkedIn account get flagged?

No. This is the entire architectural choice. Outreach automation tools (Phantombuster, MeetAlfred, Linked Helper, Dux-Soup) script clicks and connection requests inside LinkedIn — that's exactly the behavior LinkedIn detects and bans for. Riffly reads the profile you're already viewing and outputs text. It never types into LinkedIn's message UI, never clicks send, never crawls connections or activity feeds in the background. You copy and paste manually. There's no automated behavior for LinkedIn to act on. We're in the same operating zone as Apollo, Crystal, and ContactOut — read-only on LinkedIn, drafting tool that lives next to it.

How is this different from pasting a profile into ChatGPT?

Three real differences. (1) Riffly parses the profile in front of you — recent posts, pinned repos, past roles, skills — without you copy-pasting any of it. ChatGPT only sees what you bother to paste. (2) The prompt bans 40+ corporate-template phrases ("I came across", "great fit", "passion for", "scaling challenges", "thought you'd be perfect") that ChatGPT's default output reaches for. (3) Three variants per call — opener, follow-up, breakup — so the full sequence is ready in one click instead of three sessions and three different drafts.

Will the messages sound like AI wrote them?

Most LLM outreach is recognizable because it pulls from the same template-y phrase pool. The prompt blacklist exists to break that. The other half is the input: when you paste a recent post they wrote and the model has something concrete to reference, the output stops sounding generic. That's why the popup nudges you for a recent post and why the difference in quality is dramatic when you do.

What happens after my 3 free drafts this week?

The popup tells you you're at the limit. If you haven't already, share a roast on rifflylabs.com/roast for a one-time +3 bonus drafts. After that, upgrade to Pro at $15/mo for 200 drafts/month. The free tier is meant to be enough to send a handful of real messages and decide whether the replies you get back are different enough to be worth paying for.

What happens to the candidate's profile data?

It's sent to our backend, used for that single generation, and not stored. We log only that a generation happened and your account ID for billing and quota. Profile content, candidate names, and the generated messages are never persisted. Full sub-processor list, retention policies, and security posture: rifflylabs.com/security.

Does it work on Sales Navigator and LinkedIn Recruiter?

The extension can detect those surfaces and read the visible profile content. Whether you're permitted to use a third-party tool while logged into Sales Navigator or LinkedIn Recruiter depends on your seat type, your employer's agreement with LinkedIn, and your internal policies — that's a question for your manager or your legal team, not us. We never automate any LinkedIn action regardless of which surface you're on.

What if a draft is bad?

Click Generate again — the marginal cost on our side is fractions of a cent. The most common fix: paste a recent post into the optional field. Output quality lifts noticeably when the model has substance to reference.

Does this work for sales or fundraising outreach?

Yes, same prompt approach holds — describe what you're pitching and pick a tone. v1's prompt is tuned for technical recruiting; a sales-tuned variant ships next quarter.
