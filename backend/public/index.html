From a candidate's profile to a sequence ready to send.

No imports, no CRM, no API keys. Three steps in fifteen seconds.

Open a profile

Works on linkedin.com, Sales Navigator, and LinkedIn Recruiter. Click the Riff icon. The profile is auto-detected.

Write one sentence

Describe what you're hiring for. Pick a tone — warm, direct, or cheeky. Optionally paste a recent post they wrote.

Three drafts, your call

Cold opener, follow-up, breakup. Edit, copy, send manually. Mark replies as they come in to track which tones are working.

What makes the drafts different.

Four things, baked into the prompt that runs every generation.

It reads what they wrote

Headline, current role, About — pulled from the profile in front of you. A pasted recent post lifts output more than any other input. The model has substance to reference, not just job titles.

A blacklist of dead phrases

"I came across your", "great fit", "passion for", "scaling challenges" — the corporate template language a model is otherwise prone to. Banned at the prompt layer, sanitized in the output.

Conversation-starter framing

One specific observation. One concrete reason it might matter to them. One question that isn't a yes/no trap. The opener doesn't pitch — it starts a conversation that earns the pitch.

No automation, ever

Riff reads what's on screen and produces text. It never types into LinkedIn, never clicks send, never crawls in the background. You manually copy and send. That's the whole product.

Questions.

Will my LinkedIn account get flagged?

Riff reads the profile you're already viewing and outputs text. It never types into LinkedIn's message UI, never clicks send, never crawls connections or activity feeds in the background. You copy and paste manually. There's no automated behavior for LinkedIn to act on.

How is this different from pasting a profile into ChatGPT?

The prompt has a phrase blacklist that bans corporate-template language ("I came across", "great fit", "passion for") and a conversation-starter structure that ChatGPT's default output won't reach. It also returns three variants in a single call — cold opener, follow-up, breakup — so a full sequence is ready in one click instead of three sessions.

What happens to the candidate's profile data?

It's sent to our backend, used for that single generation, and not stored. We log only that a generation happened and your account ID for billing and quota. Profile content, candidate names, and the generated messages are never persisted.

Does it work on Sales Navigator and LinkedIn Recruiter?

Yes. The extension auto-detects which surface you're on and reads the right fields. If a field doesn't detect cleanly on a particular profile, ping us and we'll patch within a day.

What if a draft is bad?

Click Generate again — the marginal cost on our side is fractions of a cent. The most common fix: paste a recent post into the optional field. Output quality lifts noticeably when the model has substance to reference.

Does this work for sales or fundraising outreach?

Yes, same prompt approach holds — describe what you're pitching and pick a tone. v1's prompt is tuned for technical recruiting; a sales-tuned variant ships next quarter.
